This is a recipe for salsa that I've found to be a perfect mix of salty and spicy, which is also not overwhelmingly tomato-y.

Ingredients

  • 1 Large can of crushed tomatoes
  • 1 Large can of El Pato tomato sauce
  • 1/2 White onion
  • 1 Head of cilantro
  • 1 Lime
  • 3 Serrano peppers
  • 3 Jalapenos
  • 1 Tsp oil
  • Salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a small pan, spray about 1 tsp of your preferred oil so it coats the pan evenly and then, on a low/meidum heat, add all of your serrano peppers and jalapenos. Cook the peppers unitl a slight char appears on the entire surface.
  2. While the peppers are charring, take a white onion, cut it in half, dice it and then put it into a large bowl. This bowl will be used for the entire salsa.
  3. Once the peppers are done, remove them from pan, dice them however you prefer and then throw into bowl. I like to keep the seeds in, but you can remove if you'd like.
  4. Take your cilantro and make sure it is rinsed thoroughly. Once rinsed, fold the entire head together evenly and chop finely then throw into bowl.
